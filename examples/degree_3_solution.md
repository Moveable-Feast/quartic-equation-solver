Solve:
$$
x^{3}-6x^{2}+11x-6 = 0
$$
Coefficients:
$$
\begin{cases}
    a = 1 \\
    b = -6 \\
    c = 11 \\
    d = -6
\end{cases}
$$
Parameters:
$$
\begin{cases}
    \alpha = \dfrac{bc}{6a ^ 2} - \dfrac{b ^ 3}{27a ^ 3} - \dfrac{d}{2a} = 0 \\
    \beta = \dfrac{c}{3a} - \dfrac{b ^ 2}{9a ^ 2} = -0.3333 \\
    \Delta = \alpha ^ 2 + \beta ^ 3 = -0.037
\end{cases}
$$
Solutions:
$$
\begin{cases}
x_1 = \dfrac{-b}{3a} + \sqrt[3]{\alpha + \sqrt{\Delta}} + \sqrt[3]{\alpha - \sqrt{\Delta}} = 3 \\
x_2 = \dfrac{-b}{3a} + \omega\sqrt[3]{\alpha + \sqrt{\Delta}} + \overline{\omega}\sqrt[3]{\alpha - \sqrt{\Delta}} = 1 \\
x_3 = \dfrac{-b}{3a} + \overline{\omega}\sqrt[3]{\alpha + \sqrt{\Delta}} + \omega\sqrt[3]{\alpha - \sqrt{\Delta}} = 2
\end{cases}
$$

where
$$
\omega = \dfrac{-1 + \sqrt{3}i}{2}.
$$
